Available Datasets

Below are the datasets generated and used in this project:

  • Full Energy Data: This comprehensive dataset includes hourly energy usage data collected across all dorms at Bates College over a span of 5 years (2018–2024). The data captures detailed energy consumption patterns and includes the following columns:

    • Timestamp: The exact date and time when the energy usage was recorded.
    • Dorm: The dormitory where the energy usage occurred (e.g., Smith Hall, Adams Hall).
    • Appliance: The type of appliance consuming energy (e.g., Phones, Fridges, Laptops).
    • Season: The season during which the usage occurred (Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall).
    • Usage (kWh): The amount of energy consumed in kilowatt-hours in year.

    This dataset is ideal for exploring overall energy consumption trends and patterns.

    Download Full Dataset

  • ML-Ready Energy Data: A cleaned and processed version of the dataset prepared for machine learning tasks. This dataset simplifies the data and includes the following columns:

    • Dorm: The dormitory where the energy usage occurred.
    • Appliance: The type of appliance consuming energy.
    • Season: The season during which the usage occurred.
    • Hour: The hour of the day when the energy was consumed (0–23).
    • Month: The month when the energy was consumed (1–12).
    • DayOfWeek: The day of the week when the energy was consumed (0 = Monday, 6 = Sunday).
    • Usage (kWh): The amount of energy consumed in kilowatt-hours.

    This dataset is designed for building machine learning models to predict energy consumption based on various factors.

    Download ML-Ready Dataset

  • Visualizations: A set of pre-generated visualizations providing insights into energy consumption trends and patterns. The visualizations include:

    • Total Energy Usage by Dorm: A bar chart showing the total energy consumption for each dorm over the entire dataset.
    • Energy Usage Over Time: A line chart highlighting the energy usage trends across all dorms on a daily basis.
    • Appliance Usage by Dorm: A bar chart displaying the energy consumed by different appliances in each dorm.
    • Peak Usage Times by Dorm: A line chart showcasing the average energy usage at different times of the day for each dorm.

    These visualizations help identify key patterns and anomalies in energy usage.
